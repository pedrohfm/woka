Concept

Inhabiting

The name Woka derives from the cybertupi wiki (open to) and oca (indigenous house or dwelling). It is an open construction system that updates the building tradition of indigenous people in which residents design and build their own homes. Based on a DIY (Do it Yourself) logic, it is a process that allows anyone to design, download, print or cut, and finally, build or tweak their house or components. The idea is to generate an inner shell that is immediately deployable, combined with a more durable outter layer that can be added later on using conventional building systems available at the site. In that way people can create an initial proposal to respond to their immediate needs, change and adapt their design on site by reconfiguring the parts as needed, and use their own building culture and collective knowledge to finish the construction. In such way the system enables the dweller to add his experience to the design, based on his own culture and different ways of inhabiting.
